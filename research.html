Research

Questions

Should this be a website vs. an app?

More than one person questioned this. To add to the confusion two college students said they would prefer an app. Feedback from a UX instructor asked what if the student isn’t on a mobile phone, but using a computer in the library.
Additional research with an executive at a hunger non-profit reiterated the stigma surrounding poverty and hunger. Would a student be embarassed if an app was on their phone?

What is the problem I’m really trying to address?

A conversation with a friend who works as a consultant identified that the issue I was describing was not about hunger per se but about how to connect college students to the great resources that already exist.

User testing during Covid-19 restrictions?

“5 is still the golden rule. Limit testing to 5 users and you’ll uncover the majority of problems that plague your website or app, while still keeping your costs low and the process simple.” I struggled to hit the magic number, typically having 3 testers.

Why 5 is the magic number for UX usability testing, Ellie Martin https://www.invisionapp.com/inside-design/ux-usability-research-testing/

Solution

My Capstone project started out as an app to connect college students who are experiencing food insecurity with existing campus food pantries and/or community fridges stocked with fresh, healthy food. is has changed to be a mobile rst website in response to research with college students and people involved in hunger initiatives. A number of factors played into this decision, including the lower cost of owning a phone, accessibility for students who might be using a computer in the library, and the stigma of hunger and poverty that might inuence a student’s willingness to download an app on their phone. I’m focusing on the user experience process to create a website that is simple and easy to use, limiting the number of screens to avoid “losing” a user in this critical path.

A secondary component of the website will be a way for the community to support existing campus food pantries; whether its their alma mater, a local food pantry, or one where they have a personal connection; with donations of food, money or time - ultimately creating hope around the issue of college hunger.
