Summary

Final Deliverable

My Capstone project was created to connect college students who are experiencing food insecurity with campus food pantries and community fridges stocked with fresh, healthy food.

Through the user experience process of research, testing, and design, the Food For Thought mobile website is a simple and efficient way for students to achieve this goal.

The secondary component for the community to support existing campus food pantries; whether its their alma mater, a local food pantry, or one where they have a personal connection; with donations of food, money or time; is also simple and efficient.

Going forward, I don’t know where this may live. I contacted FoodFinder and the College and University Food Bank Alliance, thinking that combining the existing CUFBA database with FoodFinder's search function was an obvious way to collaborate, creating a site specifically for college students. I still believe that a college-specific application is relevant given the insular nature of the college campus community and would love to see this project become a reality.

Thoughts on the impact of Covid-19 on college students. There is a significant increase in need; students are facing lost income, additional expenses for remote learning, and have been excluded from many of the support programs. In addition to campus closings, students’ location is in flux - on campus, off campus, home.

When I started the project, 2017 data indicated 36% of college students were food insecure. In the 2019 #RealCollege survey that number increased to 39%. Food for thought.
